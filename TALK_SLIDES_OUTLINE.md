## Slide 1 - Why This Matters

Slide content:
- Literature volume is growing faster than manual review can handle
- AI can accelerate search, screening, and synthesis
- Speed without rigor is risky: we need transparent workflows

Speaker notes:
- Open with the pain point: too many papers, too little time.
- Set the framing: AI is a force multiplier, not a replacement for research judgment.
- Promise practical workflows and safeguards.

## Slide 2 - Talk Roadmap

Slide content:
- 1) Find a few good papers quickly
- 2) Build a systematic search strategy with AI
- 3) Synthesize evidence effectively
- 4) Build citation/literature maps
- 5) Compare useful AI tools

Speaker notes:
- Tell the audience this is a full pipeline from question to synthesis.
- Mention that examples can be adapted to any field.

## Slide 4 - Finding a Few Good Papers with AI

Slide content:
- Start with seed terms and ask AI for synonyms/related concepts
- Query trusted indexes (PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, Semantic Scholar)
- Ask AI to prioritize by relevance, recency, and method quality
- Manually validate top candidates

Speaker notes:
- Emphasize this is a rapid scoping step, not full systematic review yet.
- Show a quick example prompt and resulting top-paper shortlist.

## Slide 5 - Build a Systematic Search Strategy

Slide content:
- Convert question into concept blocks (PICO or equivalent)
- Expand each block with synonyms and controlled terms (e.g., MeSH)
- Construct Boolean search strings
- Translate syntax per database

Speaker notes:
- Show one concept block and how AI expands it.
- Stress database-specific adaptation to avoid missing relevant studies.

## Slide 6 - Reproducibility and Search Log

Slide content:
- Save every query version
- Record date, database, fields searched, and hit counts
- Pilot against known key papers
- Adjust sensitivity vs precision

Speaker notes:
- Explain that reproducibility is what makes AI-assisted work defensible.
- Mention PRISMA-style reporting expectations.

## Slide 7 - AI-Assisted Screening and Extraction

Slide content:
- Triage titles/abstracts with AI support
- Use a structured extraction template:
  - Design, sample, intervention/exposure, outcomes
  - Main findings, limitations, bias notes
- Keep human-in-the-loop decisions

Speaker notes:
- Clarify where AI can help (consistency and speed).
- Clarify where humans must decide (eligibility and interpretation).

## Slide 8 - Evidence Synthesis Workflow

Slide content:
- Build comparison tables across studies
- Use AI to draft thematic synthesis
- Verify all quantitative claims against sources
- Explicitly report uncertainty and contradictions

Speaker notes:
- Demonstrate a before/after of raw extraction to synthesis paragraph.
- Warn against trusting generated summaries without source checks.

## Slide 9 - Citation and Literature Mapping

Slide content:
- Backward citation search: references in key papers
- Forward citation search: newer papers citing key papers
- Use graph tools to find clusters, hubs, and gaps
- Turn map into story: foundations, frontier, blind spots

Speaker notes:
- Show how maps help identify influential and overlooked work.
- Tie this to future research directions.

## Slide 10 - Tool Landscape

Slide content:
- Discovery/graph: Semantic Scholar, Connected Papers, Research Rabbit
- Systematic review support: ASReview, Rayyan
- Synthesis support: GPT-style assistants, Claude, Perplexity (with source verification)
- Reference management: Zotero-based workflows

Speaker notes:
- Present tools by job-to-be-done, not hype.
- Mention trade-offs: coverage, transparency, export options, cost.

## Slide 11 - Risks, Limitations, and Guardrails

Slide content:
- Hallucinated citations and incorrect claims
- Bias amplification from training data and retrieval filters
- Over-reliance on convenience rankings
- Guardrails: source verification, logs, dual review for critical steps

Speaker notes:
- Highlight that methodological discipline matters more than model choice.
- Give one concrete failure example and mitigation.

## Slide 12 - Practical Takeaways

Slide content:
- AI can cut time for discovery and synthesis prep
- Quality depends on reproducible methods and verification
- Adopt a pipeline: define, search, screen, extract, synthesize, map
- Start small: one topic, one reproducible workflow

Speaker notes:
- End with actionable guidance.
- Invite audience to adapt the template to their own domain.
